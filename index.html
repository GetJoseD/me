Small-business security — before & after
Before — fragile & personal
  • A mix of email accounts — some named for the business, some personal Gmail — with work and personal data intermingled.
  • Multi-factor authentication tied to one person's personal phone, using text-message codes.
  • Access couldn't be shared safely; a single staffing change could lock the business out of its own accounts.
  • Nothing could grow without being rebuilt from scratch.
Now — separated & built to scale
  • One dedicated business email, with personal accounts kept fully separate.
  • MFA moved to Google Authenticator instead of SMS — so access can be shared securely, not trapped on one phone.
  • Migrating to Google Workspace with a real business-domain address and separate managed accounts for devices, staff, and roles.
  • Systems that scale as the business grows — no more starting over.
In progress — rolling out in stages so day-to-day operations never stop.
Systems & integration — scattered to scalable

When I started helping them, every ordering service ran on its own separate device — and keeping them all running and updated was a constant fight. Multiple delivery apps, plus the POS, plus Google: a single menu price change meant logging into each system by hand.

Where they started
  • A separate device for each delivery service, alongside the POS and the Google listing.
  • One small price change could take an hour or more — updated system by system, by hand.
  • Fragile, easy to miss a spot, and nearly impossible to keep consistent.
Where they are now
  • We use Shift4 to power all of their delivery menus from one place — update once, push everywhere.
  • Fewer devices, one source of truth, and far less daily maintenance.
What's next
  • Integrate Owner.com so menu updates flow through automatically.
  • Better menu-item photography, so the listings actually sell.
  • Managed sales campaigns across the delivery services to win new and repeat customers.
  • QR-code ordering and payment to offset low staffing — plus a drinks-focused kiosk.
Why it mattersMost owners who adopt systems one at a time never integrate them — they don't know how, or they're afraid to break something that already works, even when it's inefficient. Closing that gap is the whole point.
