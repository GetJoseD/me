Bringing a little of Vietnam into the room — on a budget.

The ownership here is deliberately budget-conscious — and that's not a limitation so much as a design brief. Because we source cultural pieces online at low cost and buy in batches, a tight budget still adds up to a space with real character.

The ceiling tells the story best. The room came with a standard corporate drop-ceiling and hard fluorescent lighting, and the landlord won't replace the tiles or swap the fixtures. So we hung Asian-themed cloth umbrellas across it — they hide the grid overhead and diffuse the harsh light into something warm. Vietnamese silk lanterns hang alongside them, adding color and a sense of place.

The idea is simple: bring little pieces of Vietnam into the restaurant, one affordable, intentional piece at a time. A room people want to photograph and share is free, long-term marketing — and there's more planned as the budget allows.
